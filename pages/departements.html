ICT Commission

The ICT commission is part of fellowship service department, it is the glassdoor for the church community all around the globe by which everyone can follow the church events from which ever corner or continent. It is composed by five subcommisions which work together to keep the community connected to the church, these include;include; projection, camera and video, live streaming,social media and writers,Development and database team, spot and church news department.

  • Projection team : This team is charge of formatting, organizing and charing the sermon text and scriptures into a more readable and appealing format.
  • Camera, Video and streaming team : This team is in charge of capturing images of every important moment and sharing them to different streaming platforms.
  • Socio media & writers team : This team is in charge of writing summaries, magazines and brochures about church events. They also interact with people on different social platforms like Facebook and Twitter.
  • Development and database Team : This team is in charge of developing software solutions that helps the church to interact better with it members through a database.
  • Spot and announcement Team : This team is charge of creating video content for church announcements and events documentaries.

Children/Sunday School Ministry

Couples Ministry

Our theme as a ministry is to grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord Jesus as the cornerstone of marriage. The ministry provides married couples with the opportunity to fellowship and learn what God ordained for marriage.

Couples participate in services, teachings, workshops and other activities designed to address different topics faced by couples, such as spiritual life, communication, conflict resolution, finances, parenting, and intimacy, just to name a few. Married along with engaged couples are invited for these events. Emphasis is always placed on the importance of growing our knowledge of God, His Word and building covenant marriage relationship.

Every third Sunday of the month, couples hold their fellowship service and all marrieds and engaged couples are welcome.

DISCIPLESHIP CLASS

The reason being of Discipleship Classes and Commissioning of Disciples is to train and to equip people who accepted Jesus as their Lord and Savior through teachings which are categorized into seven stages/ classes. Upon completion of the classes, the graduates are commissioned to be disciples into the world to preach and spread the Word of God. In the last 15 years, discipleship classes changed life and restored broken hearts of many. Disciples were trained and equipped and amongst them, pastors have been commissioned. The classes have a flexible program with an intake of Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday on top of Week-end program. For those who cannot make it to either program, the discipleship department offers a one-on-one special program.

HOME CELL

This department is composed of home cells which are grouped in 14 sections, social commission, and Masoro Community Empowerment Home cell fellowship in ERC Masoro was established in 2003 with just a few members, and in the same year, the former ERC Remera was born as the result of first home cell expansion. In 2004 the HC were increased in number, and then church leadership initiated Home cell department.

Until today, the church members gathers in small groups (Home Cells) and fellowship together. We manage, care, support and empower church members in different aspects to make the family spirit flow, and hence build a strong Christ deep-rooted love among the Christians. Our ultimate vision is to maintain the family spirit among the church members, and support people to become a great army of Christ through discipleship teachings.

OUR MISSION

  • To share the word of God among the church members
  • Evangelize the local community surrounding our home cells
  • Give care and orientation to new church members
  • Sharpening each other through testimonies, prayer, Agape, and member visitation
  • Care and support the needy (widows, orphans, poor, sick people, and prisoners)
  • Empower masoro community to become self- reliance
